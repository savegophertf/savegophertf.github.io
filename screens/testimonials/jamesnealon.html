Dear President Paxson,

I am writing to express my anger at your decision to eliminate the most diverse sports team at Brown, and my extreme frustration at the sloppy manner in which you announced it. As a member of the Brown community since 1973, and a former member of the Track and Cross Country teams, I find your decision to be silly and the manner in which it was communicated to be unworthy of the institution, and of its President. It was said of Warren G. Harding that his speeches were like armies of words, marching across the page in search of meaning. That was my reaction to the story in the BAM and your apparent reasons for eliminating the program. I read it three times in an effort to understand the justification for cutting Track and Field and Cross Country, and unfortunately your reasoning is still not clear. Of all teams at Brown, Track and Field is uniquely diverse and inclusive, low-cost on a per-capita basis, successful at a regional and national level, and completely integrated with the women's team. In fact, as they will all tell you, they are one team.

Finally, I find it appalling that members of the team found out about the decision, apparently in the works for years, at the same time as the public. I hope all of those student athletes use their great talent to sprint for the door and find a school and a school president that treats them with respect. Please count me a former member of the Brown community.

James D. Nealon '77 Exeter, New Hampshire



Jim Nealon served for 33 years as a Foreign Service Officer, including as Ambassador to Honduras, Deputy Chief of Mission in Canada, Peru and Uruguay, and as Civilian Deputy to the Commander at U.S. Southern Command. Recently he served as Assistant Secretary for International Affairs at the Department of Homeland Security, and Senior Official Performing the Duties of the Under Secretary for Policy.
